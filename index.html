⚡ Stereoelectroencephalography 🩺 ~ericoc/sEEG 🧠

These are details, images, and documentation regarding my stereoelectroencephalography (sEEG, or "stereo EEG") at the Hospital of the University of Pennsylvania in early 2026.

Stereoelectroencephalography is a minimally-invasive neurological surgical procedure that allows for intracranial monitoring (recordings from the brain’s surface or deep within the brain). The implantation of electrodes during stereoelectroencephalography is performed using robotic assistance.

My stereoelectroencephalography was done by the Penn Medicine Department of Neurosurgery in order to more closely determine the source of my epilepsy (seizures) within the right side of my brain, while I stayed in-patient at their highly sophisticated Epilepsy Monitoring Unit (EMU).

I am incredibly thankful to everyone at Penn involved in my stay, procedure, and care, including the neurosurgeons, nurses, nursing assistants, doctors, physician assistants, researchers, pharmacists, nutritionists, custodians, social workers, case managers, as well as facilities, security, and transportation personnel, among others.
