Stereoelectroencephalography

This repository contains imaging and details related to my stereoelectroencephalography (sEEG, or "stereo EEG") in early 2026.

What?

Stereoelectroencephalography allows for intracranial monitoring (recordings from the brain’s surface or deep within the brain):

  • Changing the standard of care for epilepsy monitoring at Penn Medicine

Robot

The implantation of electrodes during stereoelectroencephalography is performed using robotic assistance.

Why?

My stereoelectroencephalography was performed in order to further diagnose the source of my epilepsy in the right side of my brain.

Who/Where?

The Penn Medicine Department of Neurosurgery and Epilepsy Monitoring Unit (EMU) at the Hospital of the University of Pennsylvania are extremely impressive.

Thank You!

I am incredibly thankful to everyone at Penn involved in my stay, procedure, and care, including the neurosurgeons, nurses, nursing assistants, doctors, physician assistants, researchers, pharmacists, nutritionists, custodians, social workers, case managers, as well as facilities, security, and transportation personnel, among others.
